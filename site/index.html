One definition. Both sides. Always in sync.

Building a ROM from DTH's stock pose assets is the easy part. The work piles up when you add your own content — dozens of custom morphs, each on an exact frame, and Daz and Houdini have to agree on every single number. Miss one frame and the morph quietly breaks in Unreal, stages later. The studio turns that bookkeeping into a declaration: list your morphs once, and it generates both sides.

The Daz side

A one-click apply-script builds the entire ROM in Daz Studio — every stock section and every custom morph on its exact frame — replacing the hand-built per-character setup.

The Houdini side

The matching PoseAsset import CSV, ready to drop onto the DazToHue HDA — with the Genesis 9 path validated byte-for-byte against hand-built artifacts.

In sync by construction

Frame numbers are computed, never stored. Both artifacts derive from the same definition — change the character, regenerate, and they cannot drift apart.

How it works

  1. 01

    Describe

    Pick the Genesis version, enable ROM sections, list your body morphs and expressions.

  2. 02

    Generate

    One click produces the Daz apply-script and the Houdini PoseAsset CSV.

  3. 03

    Apply

    Run the script in Daz Studio, drop the CSV into your Houdini network.

  4. 04

    Export

    Hand off to Unreal Engine — every frame already matching, by design.

From install to your first generated ROM in about 15 minutes.

About

DTH Character Studio is a free, open-source desktop app by Polynaut (MIT-licensed), built as a companion to mrpdean's DazToHue toolset. Beyond generating ROMs it manages your character library per game project, installs and maintains the DTH content in Daz and Houdini for you, and keeps itself up to date with a built-in updater.
